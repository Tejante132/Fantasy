So without further ado, here&#8217;s my list of fantasy books you&#8217;d be a fool not to read.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10669" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&#038;ssl=1" alt="" width="1024" height="90" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=400%2C35&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=768%2C67&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1536%2C134&amp;ssl=1 1536w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=2048%2C179&amp;ssl=1 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h2>FANTASY BOOKS YOU&#8217;D BE A FOOL NOT TO READ</h2><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-10705 size-large" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=1024%2C576&#038;ssl=1" alt="Fantasy Books You'd Be a Fool Not to read: The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson; The Blue Sword by Robin McKinley; Mistborn by Brandon Sanderson" width="1024" height="576" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=1024%2C576&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=400%2C225&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=768%2C432&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=1536%2C864&amp;ssl=1 1536w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?w=1920&amp;ssl=1 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><h3></h3><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">1. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/review-the-way-of-kings/">The Way of Kings</a> by Brandon Sanderson</span></h3><p>Many years ago, I distinctly remembering that I wanted to read a book in the vein of a <em>Final Fantasy </em>game. I wanted a group of unlikely heroes facing down a god and saving the world against all the odds. I stumbled across <em>The Way of Kings </em>in a used bookstore and took it home. I was completely addicted and wondered why I hadn&#8217;t been reading fantasy books all along. This was the book that changed it all for me and made me a fantasy reader. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/review-the-way-of-kings/">here</a>.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">2. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-priory-of-the-orange-tree/">The Priory of the Orange Tree</a> by Samantha Shannon</span></h3><p>I heard so many good things about <em>The Priory of the Orange Tree </em>that I was afraid that it couldn&#8217;t possibly live up to the hype for me, but it blew me away. I&#8217;ve read it multiple times and will probably read it again in the future, especially since more books in the series have been announced (I&#8217;m ecstatic). You can read my full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-priory-of-the-orange-tree/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">3. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-bone-shard-daughter/">The Bone Shard Daughter</a> by Andrea Stewart</span></h3><p>This book is one of the few that I consider absolute perfection. A powerful current of mystery and horror runs through this fast paced book and it has everything from adorable creatures, antiheroes, and powerful female characters. You can read my full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-bone-shard-daughter/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">4. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-blighted-stars/">The Blighted Stars</a> by Meghan E. O’Keefe</span></h3><p>So, technically, this is a science fiction novel, but for the sakes of this list, we will consider it space fantasy, okay? The blurb of this book was really intriguing, and though I generally prefer fantasy, I decided to give the book a shot. I was blown away by the portrayal of an excellent enemies-to-lovers romance, the horrors of technology gone wrong, action scenes, a badass female protagonist, and unique world-building. You can read my full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-blighted-stars/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">5. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-godkiller/">Godkiller</a> by Hannah Kaner </span></h3><p>I didn&#8217;t know how I would like a story that featured a Godkiller. It sounded a lot like <em>The Witcher</em>, but boy, am I glad I gave it a chance. This is another book that features an adorable creature, an antlered winged rabbit named Skedi, who just so happens to be the god of white lies. It also has great disability representation, epic battles, and philosophical questions. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-godkiller/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">6. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-sorcery-of-thorns/">Sorcery of Thorns</a> by Margaret Rogerson</span></h3><p>This is another book that I&#8217;ve read multiple times and is just really dear to me. The novel follows Elisabeth, who&#8217;s something of a battle librarian, in a world where books are magical and have the potential to destroy the entire world. It takes place in a Victorian setting as well and is filled with sorcerers, demons, and most importantly, the magic of reading. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-sorcery-of-thorns/">here. </a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">7. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-name-wind/">The Name of the Wind</a> by Patrick Rothfuss</span></h3><p>I know everyone who is into fantasy has probably already read this book, but if by some chance you haven&#8217;t, you need to do so immediately. The story is told from the perspective of an infamous kingkiller telling his never-before-told life story. His story begins with his childhood, when his family, a traveling troupe of performers, is brutally massacred by creatures of legend. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-name-wind/">here. </a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">8. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/reread-review-green-rider/">Green Rider</a> by Kristen Britain</span></h3><p>This book has all the bases of fantasy covered—evil mages, deadly magic forests, elves, supernaturally intelligent horses, a doomed romance, and a strong female protagonist who finds herself thrust into the position to save the world. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/reread-review-green-rider/">here. </a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">9. <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/407813.The_Blue_Sword?ac=1&amp;from_search=true&amp;qid=XLssLQXF9f&amp;rank=1">The Blue Sword</a> by Robin McKinley</span></h3><p>It&#8217;s been years since I&#8217;ve read this book, but it really left an impression on me as a younger reader. Though McKinley&#8217;s other work, <em>The Hero and The Crown</em>, has received a lot of praise, I always liked <em>The Blue Sword </em>better, personally. <em>The Blue Sword </em>is a young adult novel, but is definitely mature enough to be enjoyed by adults. The novel follows a young girl who feels like she&#8217;s never belonged and is kidnapped by a prince of a foreign land and how she learns to live in a different culture and comes into her own.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>10. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-mistborn/">Mistborn</a> by Brandon Sanderson</h3><p>I feel like this is a blatantly obvious choice, but there is nothing quite like <em>Mistborn</em>. The entire idea of the book revolves around the question of what would happen if the villain wins. <em>Mistborn </em>follows the fight against an all-powerful dictator that the protagonist had failed to stop previously. The novel demonstrates the dark side of heroes, a great student-teacher relationship, a complex heroine, and amazingly unique and intricate world-building. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-mistborn/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10669" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&#038;ssl=1" alt="" width="1024" height="90" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=400%2C35&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=768%2C67&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1536%2C134&amp;ssl=1 1536w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=2048%2C179&amp;ssl=1 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p>What books are on your list of fantasy books you&#8217;d be a fool not to read? I&#8217;d love to add them to my TBR!</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7684" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?resize=1000%2C300&#038;ssl=1" alt="" width="1000" height="300" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?w=1000&amp;ssl=1 1000w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?resize=400%2C120&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?resize=768%2C230&amp;ssl=1 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><div class="sharedaddy sd-sharing-enabled"><div class="robots-nocontent sd-block sd-social sd-social-icon-text sd-sharing"><h3 class="sd-title">Share this:</h3><div class="sd-content"><ul><li class="share-bluesky"><a rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer"

So without further ado, here&#8217;s my list of fantasy books you&#8217;d be a fool not to read.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10669" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&#038;ssl=1" alt="" width="1024" height="90" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=400%2C35&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=768%2C67&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1536%2C134&amp;ssl=1 1536w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=2048%2C179&amp;ssl=1 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h2>FANTASY BOOKS YOU&#8217;D BE A FOOL NOT TO READ</h2><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-10705 size-large" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=1024%2C576&#038;ssl=1" alt="Fantasy Books You'd Be a Fool Not to read: The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson; Priory of the Orange Tree by Samantha Shannon; The Bone Shard Daughter by Andrea Stewart; Godkiller by Hannah Kaner; Sorcery of Thorns by Margarey Rogerson; The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss; Green Rider by Kristen Britain; The Blue Sword by Robin McKinley; Mistborn by Brandon Sanderson" width="1024" height="576" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=1024%2C576&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=400%2C225&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=768%2C432&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?resize=1536%2C864&amp;ssl=1 1536w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/booksyoudbeafoolnottoread.png?w=1920&amp;ssl=1 1920w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><h3></h3><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">1. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/review-the-way-of-kings/">The Way of Kings</a> by Brandon Sanderson</span></h3><p>Many years ago, I distinctly remembering that I wanted to read a book in the vein of a <em>Final Fantasy </em>game. I wanted a group of unlikely heroes facing down a god and saving the world against all the odds. I stumbled across <em>The Way of Kings </em>in a used bookstore and took it home. I was completely addicted and wondered why I hadn&#8217;t been reading fantasy books all along. This was the book that changed it all for me and made me a fantasy reader. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/review-the-way-of-kings/">here</a>.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">2. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-priory-of-the-orange-tree/">The Priory of the Orange Tree</a> by Samantha Shannon</span></h3><p>I heard so many good things about <em>The Priory of the Orange Tree </em>that I was afraid that it couldn&#8217;t possibly live up to the hype for me, but it blew me away. I&#8217;ve read it multiple times and will probably read it again in the future, especially since more books in the series have been announced (I&#8217;m ecstatic). You can read my full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-priory-of-the-orange-tree/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">3. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-bone-shard-daughter/">The Bone Shard Daughter</a> by Andrea Stewart</span></h3><p>This book is one of the few that I consider absolute perfection. A powerful current of mystery and horror runs through this fast paced book and it has everything from adorable creatures, antiheroes, and powerful female characters. You can read my full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-bone-shard-daughter/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">4. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-blighted-stars/">The Blighted Stars</a> by Meghan E. O’Keefe</span></h3><p>So, technically, this is a science fiction novel, but for the sakes of this list, we will consider it space fantasy, okay? The blurb of this book was really intriguing, and though I generally prefer fantasy, I decided to give the book a shot. I was blown away by the portrayal of an excellent enemies-to-lovers romance, the horrors of technology gone wrong, action scenes, a badass female protagonist, and unique world-building. You can read my full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-the-blighted-stars/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">5. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-godkiller/">Godkiller</a> by Hannah Kaner </span></h3><p>I didn&#8217;t know how I would like a story that featured a Godkiller. It sounded a lot like <em>The Witcher</em>, but boy, am I glad I gave it a chance. This is another book that features an adorable creature, an antlered winged rabbit named Skedi, who just so happens to be the god of white lies. It also has great disability representation, epic battles, and philosophical questions. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-godkiller/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">6. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-sorcery-of-thorns/">Sorcery of Thorns</a> by Margaret Rogerson</span></h3><p>This is another book that I&#8217;ve read multiple times and is just really dear to me. The novel follows Elisabeth, who&#8217;s something of a battle librarian, in a world where books are magical and have the potential to destroy the entire world. It takes place in a Victorian setting as well and is filled with sorcerers, demons, and most importantly, the magic of reading. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-sorcery-of-thorns/">here. </a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">7. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-name-wind/">The Name of the Wind</a> by Patrick Rothfuss</span></h3><p>I know everyone who is into fantasy has probably already read this book, but if by some chance you haven&#8217;t, you need to do so immediately. The story is told from the perspective of an infamous kingkiller telling his never-before-told life story. His story begins with his childhood, when his family, a traveling troupe of performers, is brutally massacred by creatures of legend. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-name-wind/">here. </a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">8. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/reread-review-green-rider/">Green Rider</a> by Kristen Britain</span></h3><p>This book has all the bases of fantasy covered—evil mages, deadly magic forests, elves, supernaturally intelligent horses, a doomed romance, and a strong female protagonist who finds herself thrust into the position to save the world. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/reread-review-green-rider/">here. </a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">9. <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/407813.The_Blue_Sword?ac=1&amp;from_search=true&amp;qid=XLssLQXF9f&amp;rank=1">The Blue Sword</a> by Robin McKinley</span></h3><p>It&#8217;s been years since I&#8217;ve read this book, but it really left an impression on me as a younger reader. Though McKinley&#8217;s other work, <em>The Hero and The Crown</em>, has received a lot of praise, I always liked <em>The Blue Sword </em>better, personally. <em>The Blue Sword </em>is a young adult novel, but is definitely mature enough to be enjoyed by adults. The novel follows a young girl who feels like she&#8217;s never belonged and is kidnapped by a prince of a foreign land and how she learns to live in a different culture and comes into her own.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>10. <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-mistborn/">Mistborn</a> by Brandon Sanderson</h3><p>I feel like this is a blatantly obvious choice, but there is nothing quite like <em>Mistborn</em>. The entire idea of the book revolves around the question of what would happen if the villain wins. <em>Mistborn </em>follows the fight against an all-powerful dictator that the protagonist had failed to stop previously. The novel demonstrates the dark side of heroes, a great student-teacher relationship, a complex heroine, and amazingly unique and intricate world-building. Full review <a href="https://bloggingwithdragons.com/book-review-mistborn/">here.</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10669" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&#038;ssl=1" alt="" width="1024" height="90" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1024%2C90&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=400%2C35&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=768%2C67&amp;ssl=1 768w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=1536%2C134&amp;ssl=1 1536w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/dividertest2.png?resize=2048%2C179&amp;ssl=1 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p>What books are on your list of fantasy books you&#8217;d be a fool not to read? I&#8217;d love to add them to my TBR!</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?ssl=1"><img data-recalc-dims="1" loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7684" src="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?resize=1000%2C300&#038;ssl=1" alt="" width="1000" height="300" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?w=1000&amp;ssl=1 1000w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?resize=400%2C120&amp;ssl=1 400w, https://i0.wp.com/bloggingwithdragons.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/newsiggy.png?resize=768%2C230&amp;ssl=1 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></a></p><div class="sharedaddy sd-sharing-enabled"><div class="robots-nocontent sd-block sd-social sd-social-icon-text sd-sharing"><h3 class="sd-title">Share this:</h3><div class="sd-content"><ul><li class="share-bluesky"><a rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer"
